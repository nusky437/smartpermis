Speed Limits

Vitesses limites autorisées en France
⚠️
Rain Reduces Speed Limits

In wet conditions, speed limits are reduced. Motorways drop from 130 to 110 km/h, dual carriageways from 110 to 100 km/h. Visibility below 50 m: max 50 km/h everywhere.

01

Speed Zones in Towns

In Zone 30 areas (school zones, residential), maximum speed is 30 km/h. In Zones de Rencontre, limit drops to 20 km/h — pedestrians have absolute priority.

Urban Zone
02

New Drivers (Permis Probatoire)

During the 3-year probationary period, speed limits are reduced: motorways 110 km/h, dual carriageways 100 km/h, open roads 80 km/h. Stricter alcohol limits also apply.

Probationary Drivers
03

Speed Camera Zones

France has over 4,000 fixed speed cameras and mobile radar units. Camera locations are no longer displayed on GPS apps — radar detector use carries a €1,500 fine and licence suspension.

Enforcement
🍺

Alcohol & Drugs

Alcool, drogues et conduite
🚫
Zero Tolerance for Drugs

Driving under the influence of illegal drugs — cannabis, cocaine, MDMA, opiates — is a criminal offence in France regardless of quantity. Penalties include up to €4,500 fine, 3 years imprisonment, and full licence suspension.

01

Legal BAC Limit

The legal blood alcohol concentration (BAC) limit is 0.5 g/L for most drivers. Exceeding this is an offence punishable by a €135 fine and 6 penalty points.

Standard Drivers
02

Stricter Limit for New Drivers

Drivers within their 3-year probationary period must stay below 0.2 g/L. Effectively a near-zero tolerance policy to protect inexperienced drivers on French roads.

New Drivers
03

Professional & Bus Drivers

Drivers of commercial vehicles, buses, or coaches carrying passengers must also remain under 0.2 g/L. Violation results in immediate suspension and criminal prosecution.

Professional Drivers
04

Criminal BAC Threshold

A BAC of 0.8 g/L or above is a criminal offence. Penalties include up to 2 years in prison, a €4,500 fine, and automatic licence suspension with possible vehicle confiscation.

Criminal Offence
05

Random Breath Tests

Police can conduct random breathalyser tests at any time. Refusing a test is treated as a positive result and carries the same heavy penalties as drunk driving.

Police Powers
06

Breathalyser Kit

Drivers are recommended to carry a certified NF disposable breathalyser kit inside the vehicle for voluntary self-testing before driving.

Recommended
🔁

Priority Rules

Règles de priorité sur les routes françaises
ℹ️
Priorité à Droite — France's Famous Rule

"Priority to the right" is the default rule in France unless signs indicate otherwise. At unmarked intersections, always give way to any vehicle approaching from your RIGHT — even from a small side street.

Priority Road

You have priority over all vehicles joining the road

Give Way

Slow down and yield to all traffic on the main road

Stop (Arrêt absolu)

Full stop required — even if the road appears clear

Roundabout Priority

Vehicles inside have priority over entering traffic

01

Roundabout Rules

Since 1983, vehicles inside the roundabout have priority. Drivers entering must give way. Look for the "Vous n'avez pas la priorité" sign at modern roundabout entries.

Roundabout
02

Emergency Vehicles

Police, fire, ambulance, and gendarmerie with sirens/lights always have absolute priority. Pull to the right and stop immediately. Never block intersections or emergency lanes.

Always Priority
03

Pedestrian Crossings

Pedestrians who have stepped onto a crossing have absolute priority. Drivers must stop and wait. Failure to yield is a €135 fine and loss of 6 points from your licence.

Pedestrian Safety
🛠

Mandatory Equipment

Équipements obligatoires dans le véhicule
✅
Always Carry These in Your Car

French law requires specific safety equipment in every vehicle. Missing items can result in on-the-spot fines during police checks, even if you are not involved in an accident.

01

Warning Triangle

A red reflective warning triangle is mandatory. In a breakdown, place it 30 metres behind the vehicle on standard roads, further on motorways for visibility.

Mandatory
02

High-Visibility Vest

A fluorescent safety vest (gilet jaune) must be kept inside the passenger compartment — not the boot. Every person who exits the vehicle in a breakdown must wear it.

Mandatory
03

Fire Extinguisher

Not legally required for private cars but strongly recommended. It is mandatory for commercial vehicles, buses, and vehicles carrying dangerous goods (ADR transport).

Recommended
04

Valid Documents

Always carry: driving licence, vehicle registration (carte grise), valid insurance certificate (attestation d'assurance), and a valid ID or passport at all times.

Mandatory
05

Seatbelts & Child Seats

Seatbelts are compulsory for all passengers in all seats. Children under 10 must use an approved child restraint. Children under 135 cm must use a booster or child seat.

Mandatory
06

Crit'Air Vignette

In low-emission zones (ZFE) — Paris, Lyon, Grenoble — a Crit'Air pollution sticker is mandatory. Driving without one: fines of €68 (car) to €450 (commercial vehicle).

ZFE Zones
🪪

Licence & Points System

Le permis à points — système français
ℹ️
How the Points System Works

France uses a penalty point system (permis à points). New drivers start with 6 points. After 3 years without serious offences, the licence reaches its full 12 points. Lose all points and your licence is invalidated — you must redo the full test.

01

Regaining Points

You automatically regain points if you commit no offences: 1 point per year (up to max 12). Attend a voluntary road safety awareness course to recover 4 points every 2 years.

Recovery
02

Probationary Licence

New drivers hold a permis probatoire with only 6 initial points. The 3-year period has stricter rules. Accompanying driving (conduite accompagnée) grants 2 bonus starting points.

New Drivers
03

Licence Invalidation

Reaching 0 points automatically invalidates your licence. You must surrender it within 10 days, observe a 6-month minimum ban, then pass both theory and practical tests again.

Zero Points

Other Important Rules

Autres règles essentielles de conduite
01

Mobile Phone Use

Holding a phone while driving is illegal — even at a red light. Hands-free kits are permitted. Earphones covering both ears are prohibited. GPS on a phone mount is allowed but must not be touched while moving.

€135 Fine | −6 pts
02

Headlights & Visibility

Dipped headlights must be used in tunnels, poor visibility, and between sunset and sunrise. Hazard lights must be used when stopped in a breakdown or emergency situation.

Lighting Rules
03

Overtaking Rules

Never overtake at pedestrian crossings, junctions, bends, or hill crests. Always use indicators. Return to the right lane after passing. Overtaking on the right is forbidden except in slow-moving queues.

Overtaking
04

Motorway Rules

Keep right unless overtaking. Minimum speed in the left lane is 80 km/h in good conditions. Stopping on motorways is forbidden except in emergencies — use emergency bays only.

Motorway
05

Driving in Winter

From 1 November to 31 March, in 48 mountain departments, winter tyres or snow chains are mandatory. Applies to all vehicles including foreign-registered cars.

Seasonal Rule
06

Towing & Trailers

Towing speed limits are reduced: motorways 130 → 110 km/h (heavy trailers: 90 km/h), open roads 80 km/h. A certified towbar and working trailer lights and brakes are required.

Towing
